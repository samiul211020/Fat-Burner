About Me

A dietitian, medical dietitian, or dietician is an expert in identifying and treating disease-related malnutrition and in conducting medical nutrition therapy, for example designing an enteral tube feeding regimen or mitigating the effects of cancer cachexia. Many dietitians work in hospitals and usually see specific patients where a nutritional assessment and intervention has been requested by a doctor or nurse, for example if a patient has lost their ability to swallow or requires artificial nutrition due to intestinal failure. Dietitians are the only regulated healthcare professionals licensed to assess, diagnose, and treat such problems. In the United Kingdom, dietitian is a 'protected title', meaning identifying yourself as a dietitian without appropriate education and registration is prohibited by law.

Corn lovers will go crazy for these 3 summer salads. If you want to give your salad some smoky flavor, try grilling the cobs instead of boiling them. We <3 grilled corn, and the smokiness would totally compliment all the fresh vegetables and herbs you're adding to the mix. Homemade Pickles

Trim ends from cucumbers and slice into spears. Pack into 2 (16-oz. glass jar along with garlic and dill. Make brine: in a small saucepan, combine water, vinegar, and salt. Bring to a boil, stir until salt is dissolved, and remove from heat and let cool slightly. Pour over cucumbers, seal jar, and shake. Let cool completely on the counter, then refrigerate until cold. Wait at least 2 hours to eat the pickles, but the longer you wait, the more flavorful they’ll be. If you can, try waiting 24 hours.

Grilled Asparagus

Sure nice weather is great for the beach or the pool, but our minds immediately go to grilling. And though some folks like burgers or chicken, we can't get enough of grilled vegetables. Barbecuing veggies imparts even more flavor than roasting, and is usually even easier too! Not to mention you'll get some gorgeous char marks too. Few veggies grill up as nicely as asparagus. Thin stalks get crispy while thicker ones get tender. Finish with a bit of lemon or parm and you have the perfect cookout side. AND they finish cooking in under 5 minutes.
